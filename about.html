About Lochlann

The product is independent executive judgment.

Lochlann Strategies advises leadership teams at the point where growth ambition, customer reality, partner dependence, and execution risk converge.

The Firm

Built for consequential work, not consulting volume.

Lochlann works selectively with organizations facing a material pursuit, market-entry decision, growth inflection, post-award transition, or recovery challenge.

The model is deliberately senior and direct. There is no handoff from the person who assesses the issue to a junior delivery team. Advice is grounded in the buyer, competitor, partner, leadership, and execution realities that determine outcomes.

Operating Principles

What clients should expect.

Independent advice is useful only when it is direct enough to change a decision and practical enough to guide action.

Direct

No theater, inflated methodology, or false precision. The issue is stated clearly and challenged at the level it deserves.

Independent

No internal constituency, pipeline quota, or partner agenda. The recommendation can include not pursuing the work.

Accountable

No separation between the promise made during growth and the operating reality required after award.

Selective

Lochlann accepts work where senior attention can materially improve the decision or outcome.

Founder Perspective

Michael Maglothin

Michael's experience spans government requirements and acquisition, systems engineering, executive business development, capture, prime integration, technology commercialization, program execution, and company formation.

He has worked across government, Fortune 100, startup, and growth-stage environments, including Boeing, Honeywell, General Dynamics, L3 Technologies, and OSI Systems. That breadth is the basis of Lochlann's central premise: consequential growth decisions should be tested from every chair that will eventually determine success.

Mutual Fit

Not every situation requires an outside advisor.

The first discussion is designed to determine whether independent senior involvement would materially improve the decision.
