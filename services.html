What CEOs Call About

Specific problems. Material consequences.

Lochlann is brought in when the issue requires experienced judgment, direct executive engagement, and accountability for what happens next.

Should we pursue this?

Independent opportunity assessment before substantial bid and leadership investment.

  • Customer need and funding reality
  • Competitive position and incumbency
  • Win probability and investment case
  • Capability and delivery gaps
  • Partner requirements
  • Executive bid/no-bid recommendation

Can we win?

Capture leadership for strategic opportunities that cannot be managed as ordinary proposals.

  • Customer and stakeholder strategy
  • Competitive assessment
  • Win themes and discriminators
  • Teaming and partner structure
  • Executive engagement cadence
  • Proposal strategy and review

How do we enter?

Market entry for companies moving into federal, regulated, critical-infrastructure, or adjacent commercial markets.

  • Market attractiveness
  • Buyer and channel mapping
  • Offer and positioning
  • Partner ecosystem development
  • Initial opportunity pipeline
  • Investment and organizational roadmap

We won. Are we ready?

Post-award support that converts the winning promise into controlled execution.

  • Transition governance
  • PMO and review cadence
  • Partner integration
  • Customer communication
  • Staffing and delivery readiness
  • Risk, issue, and decision management

Why are we drifting?

Direct intervention when a pursuit or program has lost alignment, momentum, or credibility.

  • Independent diagnosis
  • Leadership and ownership reset
  • Partner conflict resolution
  • Customer confidence recovery
  • Decision and governance repair
  • Focused corrective-action plan

The issue does not need to fit a consulting category.

It needs to be consequential enough to warrant senior attention.
