Engagement Models

Defined structures for consequential work.

Engagements are matched to the decision, urgency, internal capability, and degree of executive ownership required.

Design principle

Structure follows the decision. The engagement expands only when broader ownership materially improves the outcome.

Six Structures

The right amount of senior involvement—clearly defined.

Each model identifies the sponsor, operating cadence, expected outputs, and decision standard before work begins.

Focused Review

Executive Opportunity Assessment

For leadership teams facing a material bid, market, investment, or partnership decision.

Typical structureFocused review over approximately one to three weeks.
Executive sponsorCEO, business-unit president, chief growth officer, or board representative.
Core outputsIndependent assessment, risk map, decision logic, and executive briefing.
Best outcomeA defensible pursue, do-not-pursue, or conditional-pursuit decision.
Embedded Leadership

Capture Executive

For a strategic pursuit requiring senior ownership across customer, competition, partners, solution, price, and proposal.

Typical structureEmbedded through defined capture phases or through submission.
Executive sponsorCEO, growth executive, business-unit leader, or pursuit executive.
Core outputsCapture strategy, decision cadence, teaming structure, executive challenges, and proposal direction.
Best outcomeA credible, governed pursuit with leadership aligned on the real path to win.
Recurring Executive Support

Fractional Growth Executive

For organizations needing senior growth leadership before or instead of a permanent executive hire.

Typical structureRecurring weekly or monthly cadence, usually over several months.
Executive sponsorCEO, founder, investor, or board.
Core outputsPipeline governance, customer strategy, capture discipline, team leadership, and executive reporting.
Best outcomeA growth function with stronger choices, ownership, and operating rhythm.
Award-to-Execution

Post-Award Transition

For organizations that have won work larger, faster, or more complex than the current delivery model comfortably supports.

Typical structureTemporary leadership from award through stable execution.
Executive sponsorCEO, COO, business-unit president, or program executive.
Core outputsTransition plan, governance, staffing logic, partner integration, risk controls, and customer cadence.
Best outcomeA controlled startup that protects customer confidence and avoids preventable execution debt.
Targeted Intervention

Program or Pursuit Recovery

For situations where the organization senses drift but lacks a shared diagnosis or clear corrective owner.

Typical structureRapid diagnostic followed by a defined intervention.
Executive sponsorCEO, COO, growth executive, or board representative.
Core outputsRoot-cause assessment, corrective priorities, ownership reset, and executive action plan.
Best outcomeRestored decision discipline, accountability, and customer or stakeholder confidence.
Confidential Counsel

CEO and Board Advisory

For leaders who need an independent operating perspective without creating another internal constituency.

Typical structureDefined advisory cadence or issue-specific consultation.
Executive sponsorCEO, founder, board chair, investor, or operating partner.
Core outputsIndependent challenge, scenario assessment, decision support, and preparation for consequential meetings.
Best outcomeClearer decisions before momentum, politics, or sunk cost narrow the available options.
Operating Principle

Senior attention remains senior attention.

Lochlann does not sell executive access and substitute junior delivery. The person assessing the issue remains directly involved in the work, decisions, and executive communication.

Confidential Discussion

Start with the decision, not the package.

The initial conversation determines whether a focused review, embedded role, recurring advisory cadence, or another path makes sense.
