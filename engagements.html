Engagement Models

Defined structures for consequential work.

Engagements are matched to the decision, urgency, internal capability, and degree of executive ownership required.

Focused Review

Executive Opportunity Assessment

For leadership teams facing a material bid, market, investment, or partnership decision.

Typical structure
Focused review over approximately one to three weeks.
Executive sponsor
CEO, business-unit president, chief growth officer, or board representative.
Core outputs
Independent assessment, risk map, decision logic, and executive briefing.
Best outcome
A defensible pursue, do-not-pursue, or conditional-pursuit decision.
Embedded Leadership

Capture Executive

For a strategic pursuit requiring senior ownership across customer, competition, partners, solution, price, and proposal.

Typical structure
Embedded through defined capture phases or through submission.
Executive sponsor
CEO, growth executive, business-unit leader, or pursuit executive.
Core outputs
Capture strategy, decision cadence, teaming structure, executive challenges, and proposal direction.
Best outcome
A credible, governed pursuit with leadership aligned on the real path to win.
Recurring Executive Support

Fractional Growth Executive

For organizations needing senior growth leadership before or instead of a permanent executive hire.

Typical structure
Recurring weekly or monthly cadence, usually over several months.
Executive sponsor
CEO, founder, investor, or board.
Core outputs
Pipeline governance, customer strategy, capture discipline, team leadership, and executive reporting.
Best outcome
A growth function with stronger choices, ownership, and operating rhythm.
Award to Execution

Post-Award Transition

For organizations that have won work larger, faster, or more complex than the current delivery model comfortably supports.

Typical structure
Temporary leadership from award through stable execution.
Executive sponsor
CEO, COO, business-unit president, or program executive.
Core outputs
Transition plan, governance, staffing logic, partner integration, risk controls, and customer cadence.
Best outcome
A controlled startup that protects customer confidence and avoids preventable execution debt.
Targeted Intervention

Program or Pursuit Recovery

For situations where the organization senses drift but lacks a shared diagnosis or clear corrective owner.

Typical structure
Rapid diagnostic followed by a defined intervention.
Executive sponsor
CEO, COO, growth executive, or board representative.
Core outputs
Root-cause assessment, corrective priorities, ownership reset, and executive action plan.
Best outcome
Restored decision discipline, accountability, and customer or stakeholder confidence.
Confidential Counsel

CEO and Board Advisory

For leaders who need an independent operating perspective without creating another internal constituency.

Typical structure
Defined advisory cadence or issue-specific consultation.
Executive sponsor
CEO, founder, board chair, investor, or operating partner.
Core outputs
Independent challenge, scenario assessment, decision support, and preparation for consequential meetings.
Best outcome
Clearer decisions before momentum, politics, or sunk cost narrow the available options.
Operating Principle

Senior attention remains senior attention.

Lochlann does not sell executive access and substitute junior delivery. The person assessing the issue remains directly involved in the work, decisions, and executive communication.

Confidential Discussion

The initial conversation determines whether a focused review, embedded role, recurring advisory cadence, or another path makes sense.
