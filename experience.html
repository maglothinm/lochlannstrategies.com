Career Arc

Experience translated into a sharper decision system.

Each perspective adds a different test: what the customer will believe, what the company can finance, what partners will execute, and what delivery will require after award.

  1. 01

    Government Customer and Requirements Owner

    Created requirements, acquisition materials, and evaluation structures from the customer side.

    This perspective informs how buyers define need, test credibility, weigh risk, and distinguish an internally persuasive presentation from an externally compelling offer.

    Client value: More realistic customer strategy, stronger qualification, and fewer assumptions about how procurement decisions are actually made.
  2. 02

    Growth and Capture Executive

    Led complex business-development and capture activity across strategic, partner-dependent, and high-consequence opportunities.

    Responsibilities extended beyond pipeline reporting into customer engagement, competitive strategy, teaming, internal approvals, solution alignment, and executive bid decisions.

    Client value: Pursuit discipline grounded in the entire decision system—not proposal activity alone.
  3. 03

    Prime Integrator and Teaming Leader

    Built and managed formal teams involving major primes, specialized firms, technology providers, diligence, approvals, negotiated roles, and workshare.

    The central challenge was not assembling logos. It was creating a team the customer could believe and the partners could actually execute.

    Client value: Better partner selection, clearer incentives, stronger governance, and fewer execution problems hidden inside the capture plan.
  4. 04

    Program and Execution Leader

    Worked where commitments made during growth became staffing, technology, schedule, governance, and customer-accountability obligations.

    This includes experience in high-consequence mission environments where execution failure has operational, financial, security, and reputational effects.

    Client value: Advice that tests not only whether an opportunity can be won, but whether the organization can responsibly deliver it.
  5. 05

    Founder and Technology Commercializer

    Experienced the constraints of building, positioning, partnering, and selling without the institutional advantages of a major corporation.

    That perspective sharpens the difference between theoretically attractive markets and routes to revenue that a smaller organization can actually finance and execute.

    Client value: Market-entry and growth advice calibrated to real organizational capacity, not generic corporate strategy.
